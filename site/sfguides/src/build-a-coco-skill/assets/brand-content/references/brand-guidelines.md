# Peak Outfitters – Brand Voice & Style Guidelines

This is the on-demand reference for the `brand-content` skill. It is intentionally short
so it is cheap to load. Marketing owns this file and updates it as the brand evolves.

## Voice

- **Confident, not boastful.** State what the product does and let it stand.
- **Plainspoken.** Short sentences. No jargon, no buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "best-in-class").
- **Outdoors-forward.** Ground examples in real use on the trail, water, or mountain.

## Approved terminology

| Use | Don't use |
|-----|-----------|
| gear | equipment, product SKUs |
| adventurers, customers | users, consumers |
| Peak Outfitters | the company, we (as a name) |
| built to last | durable, high-quality |

## Structure rules

- **Lead with the benefit**, then the feature. ("Stay dry through a three-day storm – our new GORE-TEX shell…")
- Keep paragraphs to **2–3 sentences**.
- Emails: one clear ask. Slide outlines: one idea per slide, max 6 slides.

## Approved brand hook

Open customer-facing content with a variation of: **"The outdoors doesn't wait – and neither do we."**

## Calls to action

- **Email CTA**: "Shop the collection" linking to the seasonal landing page.
- **Slide CTA**: a closing slide titled "Get outside" with the seasonal offer.

## Never

- Never promise specific discount percentages without sign-off from Marketing.
- Never reference competitors by name.
